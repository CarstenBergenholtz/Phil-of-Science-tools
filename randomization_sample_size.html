Pedagogical Lesson

Randomization tends to be more likely to create identical groups as N increases.

In pilot samples (N=20), "luck" plays a significant role. You might accidentally get mostly males or mostly females, purely by chance. This is called chance imbalance.

In large samples (N=150), the randomness evens out, ensuring that the groups being created are as identical as possible.

Try clicking the buttons multiple times to see how the percentages fluctuate for N=20, while N=150 stays consistent.
