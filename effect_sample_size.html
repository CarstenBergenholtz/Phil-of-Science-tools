General Take-away

Important: Just because a study shows "no significant difference" (p > 0.05), that does not mean the dice are equal. We know Die B is biased (because you set the slider!), but with small samples, science often fails to show what is actually true. This principle applies to all quantitative work: whether you are doing experiments, surveys, or other data comparisons.
